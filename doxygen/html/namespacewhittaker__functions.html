Radial wavefunctions from Whittaker's function.

The radial wavefunction of the Schrödinger equation with the Coulomb potential can be expressed in terms of the Whittaker function.

\[ R_{\nu,l}(r) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\nu^2 \Gamma(\nu+l+1) \Gamma(\nu-l)}} W_{\nu, l+1/2}\left(\frac{2r}{\nu}\right) \]

Parameters

[in]langular quantum number
[in]rradial position

Compute the Whittaker function.

The Whittaker function is defined in terms of the confluent hypergeometric function.

\[ W_{k,m}(z) = \mathrm{e}^{-z/2} z^{m+1/2} U\left(m-k+\frac{1}{2}, 1+2m, z\right) \]
