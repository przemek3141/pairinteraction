25 /** \brief Quantum defect storage
26  *
27  * The quantum defect queries a database for Rydberg-Ritz coefficients and
28  * model potential parameters. The Rydberg-Ritz coefficients are not exposed to
29  * the outside but included in the energy data member.
30  *
31  * The slight deviation from the hydrogen atom for Rydberg atoms are captured
32  * in the so-called quantum defect. The hydrogen energy is given by \f$ E =
33  * \mathrm{Ry}/n^2 \f$. For Rydberg atoms this is slightly modified to \f$ E^*
34  * = \mathrm{Ry}/n^{*2} \f$ where \f$ n^* = n - \delta_{nlj} \f$. This
35  * correction can be expanded in a series
36  * \f[
37  * \delta_{nlj} = \delta_0
38  * + \frac{\delta_2}{(n-\delta_0)^2}
39  * + \frac{\delta_4}{(n-\delta_0)^4}
40  * + \frac{\delta_6}{(n-\delta_0)^6}
41  * + \cdots \;
42  * \f]
43  * The coefficients of the series \f$ \delta_i \f$ (Rydberg-Ritz coefficients)
44  * are loaded from the database.
45  *
46  * The object also loads the parameters for the model potentials which are used
47  * in the Numerov object.
48  */
